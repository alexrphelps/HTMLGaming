# The Gravity Locksmith

The Gravity Locksmith is a precision platformer about surviving a hostile vault by flipping gravity, chaining jumps, and collecting the right shards before the exit opens.

You move through a full campaign of handcrafted rooms where every level asks you to read the route, manage your charge, and decide when to push forward or wait for a better opening.

## Core Experience

- Gravity is the main tool and the main risk.
- Short, readable rooms turn each attempt into a route-planning puzzle.
- The campaign steadily layers in tougher combinations of locks, hazards, moving platforms, and timed pressure.
- Progress is earned by collecting room shards, opening the exit, and surviving to the end of the vault.

## Controls

- Move: `A/D` or `Left/Right`
- Jump: `W`, `Space`, or `Up`
- Double jump: available in the air
- Flip gravity: `Shift`
- Restart room: `R`
- Start / continue: `Enter` or `Space`

## Gameplay Features

### Gravity Flipping

- Swap the direction of gravity to travel on ceilings as well as floors.
- Use flips to reach alternate paths, dodge hazards, and cross awkward room layouts.
- Gravity charge limits how often you can rely on this ability, so each flip matters.

### Double Jumping

- Chain a second jump in midair to extend routes or save a bad landing.
- Double jump charge is separate from gravity charge, which creates a second layer of resource management.
- Battery pickups can restore jump access and keep longer routes viable.

### Shard Collection

- Many rooms require collecting shards before the exit becomes usable.
- Shards are often placed to force riskier routes or to split the room into distinct path choices.
- Some gates and locks also depend on shard counts, so gathering them is part of opening the level itself.

### Gates and Locks

- Gates block movement until enough shards have been collected.
- Some rooms also include named locks that require a specific shard threshold.
- This creates clear room objectives beyond simply reaching the exit.

### Hazards

- Spikes punish bad landings and rushed movement.
- Enemies add contact threats and route pressure.
- Sentry hazards fire through space on a cycle, making timing and patience important.
- Chase rooms add a red-line threat that steadily advances behind the player.

### Moving Platforms

- Some rooms use moving platforms to create timed crossings and changing landing spots.
- These routes often pair with gravity flips or hazards, so the safest option is not always the fastest one.

### Room Pressure and Scoring

- Each room tracks time, deaths, and flips.
- Room results are recorded after completion.
- The game also presents par goals for time and flips, giving players a clear benchmark for cleaner runs.

## Campaign Structure

The game contains 40 rooms, starting with short teaching rooms and building into longer, more demanding vault sections.

The campaign includes:

- Introductory rooms that teach gravity flipping and battery use
- Gate and lock rooms that require shard routing
- Enemy patrol and hunter encounters
- Sentry-beam timing rooms
- Moving-platform rooms
- Chase sections that add urgency
- Final vault rooms that combine multiple systems at once

## Room Variety

Each room can mix and match different challenges:

- Platform routes
- Exit placement on either the floor or ceiling
- Shards and refill pickups
- Patrol enemies and hunter enemies
- Sentry beams
- Moving platforms
- Locked gates
- Chase pressure
- Room-specific guidance text

That variety keeps the campaign feeling like a series of vault contracts rather than a single repeated stage.

## Victory

Clear all rooms to break the vault and finish the run.

At the end of the campaign, the game shows your total deaths, total flips, total time, and a room-by-room summary of your run.

## Player Goal

The best runs are the ones where you:

- Spend gravity flips carefully
- Use the double jump to stay in control
- Collect shards in a useful order
- Treat hazards as part of the route, not just obstacles
- Reach the exit with enough momentum to keep the run clean

## Notes

- The Gravity Locksmith is designed as a focused browser platformer.
- Rooms are handcrafted to escalate in difficulty and to reward route planning more than raw speed alone.
